About APCSL

Leading research in adaptive performance and complex systems engineering

Our Mission

The Adaptive Performance in Complex Systems Lab advances understanding of how complex sociotechnical systems adapt and maintain resilient performance. We conduct cutting-edge research that bridges theory and practice, developing innovative solutions for real-world challenges in healthcare, education, and industry.

Our Vision

To be a world-leading research center in understanding and designing adaptive performance in complex sociotechnical systems, contributing to safer and more effective healthcare and educational environments while training the next generation of human factors engineers.

Collaborations

We maintain strong partnerships with healthcare institutions, educational organizations, and industry leaders. Our collaborative approach ensures that research findings translate into practical applications that improve system performance and human wellbeing.

Clemson University Affiliation

As part of Clemson University's Department of Industrial Engineering, we leverage the university's strong engineering tradition and world-class research facilities. Our lab embodies Clemson's commitment to innovative research that makes a real difference in the world.

Research Areas

Advancing knowledge in complex systems and human factors engineering

Healthcare Systems

We investigate adaptive performance in healthcare environments, focusing on patient safety, workflow optimization, and technology integration. Our research includes studies on emergency department operations, surgical team coordination, and health information systems to improve care delivery and patient outcomes.

View Publications

Resilience Engineering

Our lab develops frameworks and methodologies to enhance system resilience, enabling organizations to maintain performance under varying conditions and recover from disruptions. We focus on proactive resilience strategies, adaptive capacity building, and organizational learning.

View Publications

Cognitive Systems Engineering

We explore human-system interactions, cognitive workload, and decision-making processes to design more effective complex systems. Our work includes cognitive task analysis, situation awareness studies, and research on human-automation interaction in safety-critical domains.

View Publications

Adaptive System Design

Creating design principles and tools that enable systems to adapt dynamically to changing requirements while maintaining optimal performance. We develop adaptive interfaces, flexible workflows, and intelligent support systems for complex work environments.

View Publications

Publications

Our contributions to the scientific community

Adaptive Performance Measurement in Healthcare Teams: A Multi-Method Approach
Hegde, S., Johnson, M., Smith, K.
Human Factors, 2024

This study presents a comprehensive framework for measuring adaptive performance in healthcare teams, incorporating physiological, behavioral, and cognitive indicators.

Resilience Patterns in Emergency Department Operations During Crisis Events
Chen, L., Hegde, S., Williams, R.
Applied Ergonomics, 2023

An analysis of how emergency departments adapt their operations during high-stress crisis events, identifying key resilience factors and adaptive strategies.

Cognitive Load Assessment in Complex Sociotechnical Systems
Hegde, S., Davis, A.
Proceedings of HFES 2023

A novel approach to real-time cognitive load assessment using multimodal sensors and machine learning in complex work environments.

Design Principles for Adaptive User Interfaces in Healthcare Information Systems
Rodriguez, P., Hegde, S., Thompson, J.
International Journal of Medical Informatics, 2023

Evidence-based design principles for creating adaptive interfaces that respond to user context and workload in healthcare settings.

Latest updates from the APCSL

December 2024

NSF Grant Award

Our lab received a $500,000 NSF grant to study adaptive performance in healthcare systems during emergency situations. This three-year project will involve collaboration with Prisma Health and regional emergency services.

Best Paper Award at HFES 2024

Dr. Hegde and team received the Best Paper Award at the Human Factors and Ergonomics Society Annual Meeting for their work on "Real-time Cognitive Load Assessment in Complex Systems."

October 2024

PhD Defense Success

Congratulations to Dr. Sarah Johnson on successfully defending her dissertation titled "Adaptive Interfaces for Emergency Medical Decision Support Systems." She will be joining Microsoft Research as a postdoctoral researcher.

Dr. Hegde delivered a keynote presentation on "The Future of Resilience Engineering in Healthcare" at the International Conference on Resilience Engineering in Copenhagen, Denmark.
