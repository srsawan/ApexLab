Dr. Sudeep Hegde

Assistant Professor | Clemson University

Dr. Sudeep Hegde is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Industrial Engineering at Clemson University, where he also serves as a Faculty Scholar with the Clemson University School of Health Research (CUSHR). He earned his PhD and MS in Industrial and Systems Engineering with a concentration in Human Factors Engineering from the University at Buffalo (SUNY) and his undergraduate degree in Industrial Engineering and Management from Ramaiah Institute of Technology, Bengaluru.

Before joining Clemson, he was a Harvard Research Fellow at Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center affiliated with Harvard Medical School and later an Assistant Research Engineer and Instructional Assistant Professor at Texas A&M University. His research integrates human factors, cognitive systems engineering, and healthcare operations to study how complex sociotechnical systems, especially in healthcare, adapt, learn, and maintain resilience under stress.

Research Areas

Hover to explore our work

Proactive Organizational Learning

Developing frameworks and methods for organizations to learn from both successes and failures, enabling continuous improvement and adaptive capacity in complex operational environments.

Human-AI Teaming for Learning-at-Scale

Investigating collaborative frameworks where humans and AI systems work together to facilitate large-scale learning, knowledge transfer, and decision-making across distributed teams and organizations.

Remote Physiological Stress/Workload Monitoring

Utilizing advanced sensing technologies and data analytics to remotely assess physiological indicators of stress and cognitive workload in real-time operational settings.

Applications in Healthcare, Education, and Transportation

Translating human factors research and resilience engineering principles into practical solutions across critical domains including healthcare delivery, educational systems, and transportation safety.
